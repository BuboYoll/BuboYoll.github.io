Linear Algebra Basics: Matrices and Vectors

Published on 2025-11-28

1. Vectors and Matrices

A vector is a quantity with magnitude and direction. An $n$-dimensional vector can be represented as:

$$\vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} v_1 \\ v_2 \\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{pmatrix}$$

2. Matrix Multiplication

The product of two matrices $A$ and $B$, $C = AB$, is defined as:

$$C_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} A_{ik} B_{kj}$$

3. Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

For a square matrix $A$, if there exists a scalar $\lambda$ and a non-zero vector $\vec{v}$ such that:

$$A\vec{v} = \lambda\vec{v}$$

Then $\lambda$ is called an eigenvalue, and $\vec{v}$ is the corresponding eigenvector.
